Marie Carmel Jean

Artist from Haiti ( Mid 1960s - )

  • According to Boris Kravitz of the Haitian Art Company in Key West, Florida, Marie Carmel Jean was born in the mid-1960s. She was living in Carrefour, Haiti until her house burned down. Boris has not seen her since. [2002 conversation]

  • As seen in the images, Marie Carmel Jean's paintings are very finely executed, demonstrating skilled attention to detail. It would be a shame if she stopped painting. If anyone could provide us with additional information about this artist, we would very much appreciate it.

    • Mother and Daughter was one of 25 Haitian paintings from this collection that were exhibited in the show Life in Bold Colors at Sonoma State University, Sonoma, California in 2007.

  • En Route by Marie Carmel Jean c. 1997

    16 in x 12 in, Acrylic on Canvas, Framed

    SOLD

    • Marie Carmel Jean revisits the theme of a father taking his wife and child home (as in the biblical journey of Joseph, Mary and the baby Jesus from Nazareth to Bethlehem). Here, the father not only walks--carrying a heavy load in each arm--he also slogs through water. In this Haitian setting, geckos chase dragon flies up the trees to the left and right, and coco palms are faintly visible in the distance. (Zoom in and enjoy the exquisite details of this painting!)

      En Route was one of 25 Haitian paintings from this collection that were exhibited in the show Life in Bold Colors at Sonoma State University, Sonoma, California in 2007.
