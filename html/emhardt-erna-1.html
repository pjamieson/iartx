Erna Emhardt

Country Life and Astronauts

Country Life and Astronauts c. 1985

16 in x 12 in, Oil on a hard surface, Framed

$1,500 - To inquire about this piece, please email Patrick Jamieson

  • This painting portrays an interior scene with children playing in the left foreground, and--to the right--four people watching a space capsule ocean landing and astronaut pick-up on television. Behind the children is a large window looking out on a tranquil mountain scene with houses and grazing cows. (Or perhaps that's a painting-within-the-painting rather than a window.) The chair on the far right has a ghost image of someone seated facing the children. I cannot tell if that ghost image is intentional, or an earlier artifact of the painting that has emerged over time.

  • Condition: Good. There are some minor scratches and abrasions on the painting, most evident at bottom center.

  • Provenance: James S. Huffman Collection

    James S. Huffman had a distinguished 35 year career in the United States Foreign Service. A serious collector of primitive and naive art, he sought pieces local to the regions where he served. Huffman was posted to the U.S. Embasy in Berlin, German Democratic Republic (East Germany) in the mid 1980s.
