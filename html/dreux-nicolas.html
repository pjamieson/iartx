Nicolas Dreux

Artist from Haiti ( 10 Mar 1956 - )

  • Nicolas Dreux was born on March 10, 1956 in Port-au-Prince, Haiti. He began to paint as a student of Calixte Henri in 1973. He works with a razor blade; laying on the paint thick in spots and scraping it to tranparency elsewhere. After this he accents some elements of the painting with the corner of the blade.

  • Traumatized by the political violence in Haiti in the early 2000's, Dreux suffered a breakdown and stopped painting for several years. Since he started painting again, he has been creating wonderful pictures, sophisticated little gems.

[Biographic notes from Galerie Macondo.]

    The Celebration by Nicolas Dreux Dated 2012

    12 in x 16 in, Oil on Canvas, Self-Framed

    $350 - To inquire about this piece, please email Patrick Jamieson

    • Dreux's interior scenes are calm, cool, and comfortable. Like many of them, this one includes his trademark chairs and a pet (cat or dog?) in the foreground. Unlike most, the wall in the background is decorated with horizontal bands rather than having a window that looks out on the home's setting, but it still has an open curtain on the right side and several butterflies have found their way in and lit on the chairs. The couple portrayed appear to be dressed up and, with the woman holding a bouquet, may be celebrating their wedding or anniversary.

      Condition: Very good. The stretcher edges have been finished with acid-free white canvas tape, giving this Haitian painting a clean, modern look.

    Ra-Ra by Nicolas Dreux Dated 2011

    20 in x 16 in, Oil on Masonite, Mounted on Board

    $299 - To inquire about this piece, please email Patrick Jamieson

    • Under a towering streetlight, more than two dozen people participate in this rural Ra-Ra. (Ra-Ra is a traditional Easter-time procession/celebration in Haiti.) Dreux's magnificent trademark palm trees and geometrically rendered buildings of the village form the middle ground of this painting, and mountains beyond mountains and a sunset sky provide the background.

      Condition: Very good. The masonite this scene is painted on is not quite square; the top edge is 19 7/8 inches, while the bottom edge is 20 inches.

    Reaching Out by Nicolas Dreux Dated 2013

    8 in x 24 in, Oil on Canvas, Framed

    $295 - To inquire about this piece, please email Patrick Jamieson

    • Perhaps on a patio, a couple reach over a chair to each other while a cat sits in the foreground, and a trademark Dreux palm tree leans over the wall. (It's not always easy to tell if the pet in a Dreux painting is a cat or a dog, but the whiskers on this one says "Cat" to me.)

      Condition: Very good with a simple, thin wood frame.
