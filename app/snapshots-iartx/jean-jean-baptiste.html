Jean-Baptiste Jean

Haiti(21 Apr 1953 - 2002)

  • Jean-Baptiste Jean was born April 21, 1953 in Cap-Haitian and began to paint in 1972 as a student of Philome Obin. (In the first painting below, Jean-Baptiste Jean is seated at an easel with Philome Obin standing next to him, providing guidance.) He also studied at the Centre d'Art in Port-au-Prince, formally joining the Centre in 1973.  He is known as a Third Generation Master of the Northern School.

  • Jean-Baptiste Jean died in 2002.

Publications:

  • Franciscus, John Allen. Haiti: Voodoo Kingdom to Modern Riviera. 1980. pg. 112.

  • Nadal-Gardere, Marie-Jose and Gerald Bloncourt. La Peinture Haitienne. 1986. pg. 83.

  • AFHAC. La Peinture Haitienne: Dialogue du Reel et de l'Imaginaire. 1990. pp. 6, 82.

  • Drot, Jean-Marie. An Encounter between Two Worlds as Seen by Haiti's Artists. 1994. pp. 93, 106-7, 129, 164, 168-9, 176, 183, 193.

  • Demme, Jonathan. Island on Fire. 1997. pp. 144, 145.
